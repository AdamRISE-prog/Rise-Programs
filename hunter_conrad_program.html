8-Week Velocity Development Block
Athlete Hunter Conrad
Level Senior — Class of '26
Primary Goal Professional Baseball
Block Weeks 1–8
Schedule Note: Days are guidelines, not rules. What matters is getting the sessions in. If the schedule shifts, communicate it and we adjust. A lot of throwing can happen on the mound — be athletic, change directions, create stimulus.
Communicate Discomfort: If anything feels off — elbow, shoulder, anything — say it immediately. Do not push through pain. Velocity comes from a healthy arm.
Goals & Focus
Primary Goals
Maximum velocity — build the engine through hip load, shoulder separation, and a free arm that works for you
Add the gyro — develop arm path, supination, and feel for the new pitch alongside the velo work
Build a pro body — tissue tolerance, strength, and recovery capacity that can handle professional workloads
Stay healthy — every decision in this program protects the arm while pushing the output
Block Structure
Weeks 1–3: Mon / Wed / Fri throwing — building the base, staying in feel
Week 4–5: Mon / Tue / Thu / Fri — 4-day throwing, high intensity days introduced
Weeks 6–7: Pulldown phase — 3 then 5 pulldowns on high days, arm speed accumulation
Week 8: Full deload — the body adapts in recovery. Honor it. The velocity shows up after the rest.
Arm Care A on Tuesday · Arm Care B on Friday — strength-focused, higher weight lower volume
Program Focus Areas
01
Max Velocity
Velo is built through hip load, hip-shoulder separation, and a free arm — not effort. The plyo program and lift are both designed to build the engine that generates it.
02
Gyro Development
The gyro lives in supination and arm path. Develop the feel and spin during light-day throwing — don't force it on high-output days until the pattern is automatic.
03
Pro-Level Foundation
Professional baseball demands tissue tolerance built over months. Every lift, every arm care session, every post-throw routine is an investment in a body that lasts.
01
Isometric Foundation
Weeks 1–4 · Mon / Tue / Thu / Fri Lift · Low CNS Demand
Goal: Build joint stiffness, establish movement patterns, prime the nervous system. Low recovery cost — throwing ramp runs on top of this without conflict. Hold quality matters more than load. Weeks 1–3 throw Mon/Wed/Fri. Week 4 transitions to Mon/Tue/Thu/Fri throwing.
